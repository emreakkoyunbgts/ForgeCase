# Data Coverage Findings Report
**Date:** July 22, 2026

**Subject:** Domain x Region Proof Point Analysis Results

## Summary
Coverage and gap analysis has been completed for the current data corpus. The analysis identified specific blind spots across our Domains and Regions, as well as areas where data exists but lacks required outcomes. The visualized Coverage Map highlights significant data gaps and pinpoints our strongest coverage areas.

## Key Findings
Based on the generated Coverage Map, here are the critical insights from our data:

### A. Critical Gaps (Completely Missing Data)
The following Domain & Region combinations have no records in our database. We have zero coverage in these areas:

* **Widespread Domain Gaps:** regulatory reporting is completely missing in almost all regions (GCC, NL, TR, UK), with its only data point located in DE.

* **Weakest Region:** The DE region has a severe lack of data, showing gaps across 4 out of 5 domains (cloud & observability, core banking, data platform, and document intelligence).

* **Other Notable Gaps:** data platform is missing in DE, NL, and TR. cloud & observability is missing in DE, GCC, and UK. document intelligence is missing in DE, GCC and NL.

### B. Flagged Records (Missing Outcomes)
We have records for the following area, but it is missing its outcomes. This cannot be used as a valid proof point until the outcome is documented:

* In the UK region, our data platform record exists but lacks an outcome.

### C. Strongest Coverage
Our most reliable data, where both the record and its outcomes exist perfectly, is concentrated in:

* **Strongest Region:** TR has the highest number of valid proof points, covering cloud & observability, core banking, and document intelligence.

* **Strongest Domain:** core banking is our most well-documented domain, with healthy proof points across GCC, NL, and TR.
